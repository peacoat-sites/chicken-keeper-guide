## How to read this as a chicken keeper

A store dozen is your baseline, not your ceiling. Fresh backyard eggs routinely sell for 1.5x to 2x the supermarket average because buyers are paying for yolk quality and freshness, not just protein. When the tracker shows prices climbing, that premium usually holds or widens. When retail prices fall, farm-stand prices tend to be sticky; regulars rarely ask for a discount.

The year-over-year column is the one worth watching. Feed costs follow grain markets, and egg prices follow feed with a lag of a few months. If you see retail prices up sharply from last year, feed is usually the reason, and your production cost went up too.

One caution from experience: do not set your price off a single month. The monthly average bounces around supply shocks, and avian flu events can spike it 50 cents in a quarter. Price off the 3-month trend and you will not be repricing every week.
